Stability Under Stress

What quarterly data can (and can’t) tell us about U.S. honey bee colonies, 2016–2025.

What this data measures

This dataset tracks honey bee colonies quarterly. Colonies naturally expand and contract across seasons, and beekeepers actively manage them through replacements and renovations. The goal here is to show what the data can prove: seasonal rhythms, turnover, and how much intervention is required to maintain colonies.

How to read it

A stable total does not necessarily mean the system is effortless. The most informative views compare loss and recovery measures over time, and normalize by starting colonies to compare across seasons.

  • Seasonality: quarterly starting colonies can rise and fall naturally.
  • Turnover: losses, additions, and renovations can all be high at the same time.
  • Harder to maintain: intervention-per-loss can rise even if totals look stable.
